Who We Are

ITHCS Foundation is a registered not-for-profit organisation based in Delhi, established to strengthen trauma care, emergency response, and key health system components through an integrated and systems-based approach.

The foundation works across the continuum of care — from pre-hospital emergency services to hospital-based treatment, rehabilitation, and long-term health system strengthening.

The Challenge

India reports over 1.5 lakh deaths annually due to road traffic injuries, with a significant proportion of these deaths being preventable.

Despite improvements in ambulance services, a critical gap persists:

warningPatients are transported, but life-saving care is delayed or unavailable.
warningDistrict hospitals lack structured trauma stabilization systems.
warningBlood availability and coordination gaps delay critical interventions.
warningNo integration between first response, ambulance, and hospital care.
timer

The "golden hour" is lost

Not due to lack of resources, but due to lack of system integration.

Our Approach

ITHCS addresses this gap by focusing on systems, not isolated interventions.

We work across the continuum of care:

1

Prevention & First Response

Training community responders and strengthening on-ground response systems.

2

Emergency Transport Integration

Linking ambulance networks with trained facilities.

3

Hospital Stabilization Systems

Establishing trauma-ready units in district hospitals.

4

Blood Safety & Access

Strengthening availability, coordination, and rational use of blood in emergencies.

5

Technology & Data Systems

Enabling real-time coordination, monitoring, and decision-making.

Our Key Areas of Work

Strategic interventions designed to transform the landscape of Indian trauma and emergency medicine.

01

Corridor-Based Trauma Response Systems

Developing integrated trauma response models along high-risk highways and districts.

02

Trauma Stabilization Units (TSU)

Strengthening district hospitals to provide structured, life-saving stabilization within the first critical minutes.

03

Capacity Building & Training

Training first responders, ambulance personnel, and doctors and nurses through simulation-based, protocol-driven programs.

04

Blood Safety & Emergency Access Systems

Improving blood availability and linkage, emergency transfusion readiness, and coordination between blood banks and hospitals.

05

Digital & Data Platforms

Developing technology solutions for real-time emergency response coordination, case tracking and system performance, and data-driven planning and policy support.

Our Advisory Board

ITHCS Foundation has constituted a multi-stakeholder Advisory Board comprising experts from diverse domains including clinicians, public health professionals, policymakers, legal experts, and system specialists.

The board provides strategic guidance across all focus areas, including trauma care systems and blood safety improvement, ensuring a comprehensive and impactful approach.
